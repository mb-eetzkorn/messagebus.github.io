Segment Messages by Type

Use Channels & Sessions to separate and track different types of mail.

The key reason senders get into email trouble is because they send different types of content over the same domains and IP addresses. Using that approach, the best performance you'll get is that of your worst-performing content.

Best Practices

  1. Use one Channel per message type, such as alerts, notifications, newsletters, etc.
  2. Create Sessions to track unique categories, such as Password Alert, Low Balance Alert, etc.
  3. Learn More!

The Issue

Users react differently to all types of content. These reactions are measured by how the recipient engages with your content. By deploying all email types off of a shared IP address, the shared IP address is at the mercy of every piece of content that it deploys. In turn, the reputation of that IP address suffers greatly and all content becomes at risk for delivery issues.

{{ site.data.company.name }} Solution

Message Bus clients deploy each email type through its own dedicated IP address. By creating discrete Channels for sending distinct types of traffic, there is no cross-pollination of content across a shared IP. Therefore, should an issue occur with one Channel, it has no affect on the rest of your email program. Control your own email destiny by segmenting your message types with Message Bus email Channels and Sessions. Segment, protect, then track results of each email channel individually or in aggregate.
