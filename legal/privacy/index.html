"Cookies" are small pieces of information that a website sends to your computer while you are viewing a web site. We may use both session Cookies (which expire once you close your web browser) and persistent Cookies (which stay on your computer until you delete them) to provide you with a more personal and interactive experience on our Site. Persistent Cookies can be removed by following Internet browser help file directions. A "Flash cookie" is a small data file placed on your hard drive when you visit certain websites that use Adobe Flash. To adjust Flash cookie settings, access your Flash management tools from Adobe’s web site directly. If you choose to disable Cookies or Flash Cookies, some areas or features of our Site or Services may not work properly. We may also use web analytics tools, such as, but not limited to, Google Analytics, Alexa, and others.
      • We employ or our third party tracking utility partner may employ a software technology called clear gifs (a.k.a. Web Beacons/Web Bugs), that help us better manage content on our site by informing us what content is effective. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the movements of Web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user’s computer hard drive, clear gifs are embedded invisibly on Web pages and are about the size of period at the end of this sentence. We do not tie the information gathered by clear gifs to our customers’ Personal Data.
